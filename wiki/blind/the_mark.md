# The Mark

StubThis article is a stub. You can help Balatro Wiki by expanding it.

The Mark is a boss blind that draws all of the face cards face down. In other words, all of the cards with either a "King", "Queen", or "Jack" card rank, are drawn face down.

## Notes

- During the Mark, using Strength (Increases rank of up to selected cards by Available from start. ) to turn face cards into non-face ones, or using Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) to copy a face-down card to an upturned one will only briefly uncover it; conversely, turning a non-face card into a face card during won't make it face-down.

## Anti-Synergies

- Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) is particularly sensitive to this Boss Blind, as it starts with an Eternal Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ), meaning all cards are drawn face down.

- Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): All cards are drawn face down, making it much more difficult to assemble a sufficiently scoring hand.

## Strategies

- The Sort feature at the bottom of the screen can narrow down what the face-down cards are. Sorting by rank sorts in descending order with: King, Queen, Jack. Sorting by suit sorts in the order of Spades, Hearts, Clubs, then Diamonds, with each suit being sorted by rank.

- You can briefly see face-down cards when using certain tarot or spectral cards, e.g., The Empress (Enhances selected cards to [[Card Modifiers|]] Available from start. ) or Ouija (Converts all cards in hand to a single random [[Card Ranks|]]<br> hand size Available from start. ).

- Consider selling Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ) before entering the boss blind if your scoring relies on specific poker hands.

## Trivia

- The icon for The Mark is the Phoenician letter Taw (𐤕), which represents a mark (or crossed sticks) in the hieroglyphics from which it is derived.
