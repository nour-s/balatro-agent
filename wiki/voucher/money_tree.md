# Money Tree

()

Money Tree is a voucher that raises the cap on interest earned in each round to $20.

## Usage Notes

- The player must max out the interest per round earnings for ten consecutive rounds to unlock Money Tree.

- Seed Money () must be purchased in the same run for Money Tree to appear.

- The The Omelette ( All give no reward money<br>Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round 5x Banned Vouchers:<br><br><br><br>Banned Jokers:<br><br><br><br>) challenge bans Money Tree.

- The Rich get Richer ( cannot exceed the current <br><br>Start with <br>) challenge starts with a copy of Seed Money () and Money Tree ().

## Synergies

### Decks

- Yellow Deck ( Start with extra Discover at least items from your [[Collection|collection]] ) gives an initial boost to your economy, allowing you to hit the interest cap earlier on.

### Jokers

- To the Moon (Earn an extra of for every you have at end of round Uncommon Economy N/A (Passive) ) is the best Joker to pair with Seed Money. Its interactions with interest and Seed Money are listed earlier on this page.

- Bull ( for each you have <br> }} ): This voucher helps generate more interest each round, scaling Bull faster.

- Bootstraps ( for every you have <br> }} Have at least Jokers at the same time. ): This voucher helps generate more interest each round, scaling Bootstraps faster.

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Green Deck ( At end of each Round: <br> per remaining <br> per remaining <br> Earn no Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) and Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) disable interest, making Money Tree useless.

- Luxury Tax ( Hold cards in hand for every you have<br><br> hand size ) receives little benefit from Money Tree as in order to take advantage of its high interest, the player would want high starting money, which this deck punishes.

### Jokers

- Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ) encourages playing without earning interest, making Money Tree useless.

- Credit Card (Go up to in debt Common Economy N/A (Passive) ) enables going $20 in debt, well below the minimum interest level.

## Trivia

- The name of this voucher could be a reference and inverse of the common idiom, "money doesn't grow on trees," which refers to money being hard-earned and thus should not be wasted on needless things.
