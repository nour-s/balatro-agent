# Wraith

Wraith is a Spectral card that spawns a Rare Joker if there is room, but also sets money to $0.

## Usage notes

- Wraith will always set money to $0 even when in the negatives, removing any debt completely.

- If the player is already holding all Rare Jokers and not holding Showman ([[Jokers|]], [[Tarot Cards|]], [[Planet Cards|]], and [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear multiple times Reach Ante level Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ), the fallback Joker is Joker ( ).

- Similar to other consumables that generate Jokers, such as Judgement (Creates a random [[Jokers|]] card<br> Available from start. ) or The Soul (Creates a [[Legendary Jokers|]] Joker <br> Available from start. ), the Joker created by Wraith cannot be Perishable (), Eternal (), or Rental ().

- You cannot use Wraith if you have no empty Joker slots remaining (the Use button will be greyed out).

## Synergies

### Jokers

- If the player has Credit Card (Go up to in debt Common Economy N/A (Passive) ) and goes up to $-20 in debt, the player can gain up to $20 after using Wraith.

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Rich get Richer ( cannot exceed the current <br><br>Start with <br>) is not recommended to use Wraith on, as setting money to $0 in this deck means the player can't score at all.

- Non-Perishable ( All Jokers are Eternal Banned Jokers: , ; , , ; ; , , and <br>Banned [[Blinds]]: ) makes it very risky to use Wraith, as it spawns an Eternal () Rare Joker on this deck.

### Jokers

- It is not recommended to use Wraith if the player has Bull ( for each you have <br> }} ) and/or Bootstraps ( for every you have <br> }} Have at least Jokers at the same time. ), as setting money to $0 makes them lose their effect on score.

## Strategy

- Wraith creates a random Rare Joker if there is room and sets money to $0. This effect can potentially be life-saving due to how strong Rare Jokers can be, but may give little benefits if the Joker it generates doesn't fit into the run.

- Wraith can be used to remove debt completely, which can work surprisingly well to remove the downsides of using Credit Card (Go up to in debt Common Economy N/A (Passive) ) or The Tooth ( ) boss blind.

- If the build is already good enough on its own, it may not be wise to give up a Joker and use Wraith, because the Joker it generates may not be beneficial to the run and in turn can be run-killing.

- It is a good idea to spend down before using Wraith, which will help minimize its downside and allow you to see more shop items.

- Being a Joker-generating card, Wraith can't generate Eternal () Jokers, which might hinder its usage with Madness (When or is selected, gain and a random Joker <br> }} ), Ankh (Create a copy of a random [[Jokers|]], destroy all other Jokers <br> from copy)}} Available from start. ), or Hex (Add [[Card Modifiers#Editions|]] to a random [[Jokers|]], destroy all other Jokers Available from start. ).

- Do note that using Wraith will not always be beneficial, and will leave a dent in the economy if there aren't backups like The Hermit (Doubles money<br>)}} Available from start. ) or Temperance (Gives the total sell value of all current [[Jokers]]<br> }} Available from start. ), in which case the best move is to skip it or sell it.

## Trivia

- Wraith is defined as an apparition, or ghostly appearance of someone, usually showing up shortly before or after their death.

- Wraith appears on a product made in partnership with Fangamer called the Clairvoyance Play Mat.1] The cards levitating around the apparition appear to reference the art of the Incantation (Destroy random card in your hand, add random [[Card_Modifiers#Enhancements|]] [[Card Ranks#Number cards|]] to your hand Available from start. ) spectral card.
