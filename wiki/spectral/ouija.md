# Ouija

hand size

Ouija is a Spectral card that turns all cards held in hand into a single random rank, at the cost of -1 Hand Size.

## Usage notes

- Ouija cannot be used when there is only 1 card held in hand, which can happen if the player's Hand Size is 1 or they have destroyed cards until there is only 1 card left.

- Having a large Hand Size makes this card more useful, as more cards will be converted.

## Synergies

### Decks

- Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) has some of the strongest synergy with Ouija of any deck. Its +2 hand size allows it to hit two additional cards and also makes its drawback relatively negligible. This allows you to draw hands with fast planet scaling, such as Five of a Kind, frequently, mitigating the deck's downside of -1 Joker Slot.

- Ghost Deck ( [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear in the shop, start with a card Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) allows the player to hold a Ouija during the round more easily, letting them unlock Five of a Kind or Flush Five earlier.

 - Holding Ouija with The World (Converts up to selected cards to Available from start. ), The Moon (Converts up to selected cards to Available from start. ), The Sun (Converts up to selected cards to Available from start. ), The Star (Converts up to selected cards to Available from start. ), or Sigil (Converts all cards in hand to a single random [[Card Suits|]] Available from start. ) allows the player to easily unlock Flush Five.

### Poker Hands

- Four of a Kind, Five of a Kind, and Flush Five can be played much more consistently with the extra cards of 1 rank.

### Vouchers

- Paint Brush ( ) and Palette ( hand size Reduce your hand size down to five cards. ) allow more cards to be changed at a time and offset Ouija's downside.

### Blinds

- If the player holds Ouija in their consumable slots during The Serpent ( ) and only discards or plays 1 card at a time, they can change the rank of many cards at once.

### Jokers

- Merry Andy ( discards each round, <br> hand size Win a run in or fewer rounds Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ) and Stuntman (,<br> hand size Earn at least 100 million (100,000,000) Chips in a single hand. ) do not allow as many cards to be changed at a time and compound the hand size penalty.

### Poker Hands

- Straight and Straight Flush become less consistent with a narrower distribution of ranks.

## Strategy

- Ouija converts all cards held in hand into a random rank at the cost of 1 Hand size afterwards. This effect, while essentially being a huge gamble on most occasions, can be of great benefit if used properly and will help tremendously when used early enough, often giving a clear direction for deck-fixing later into the run.

- While being amazing when used early, Ouija falls off surprisingly quickly if it's found too late into the run. Most builds aim towards a few specific ranks to work, and Ouija having a (fixed) 1/13 chance for the desired rank can mess that up if not used with caution.

- The -1 Hand size given from using Ouija can be detrimental in a lot of cases, and as such, it is advised not to use Ouija when combined with other Hand size deduction effects such as Stuntman (,<br> hand size Earn at least 100 million (100,000,000) Chips in a single hand. ) or Ectoplasm (Add [[Card Modifiers#Editions|]] to a random hand size Available from start. ).

## Trivia

- A Ouija board is a board with alphabetical letters and numbers on it and was believed to have been used to converse with spirits of those who have passed away.

- The numbers and letters on the card's art are the ranks of playing cards: A=Aces, 2-10=Rank of 2 through 10, J=Jack, Q=Queen, K=King.

 - Each of the cards' ranks on the Ouija board are also highlighted in a different color. Aces are colored white, numbered cards (2-10) are colored saturated gold, and face cards (King, Queen, and Jack) are dark blue.

- Sigil (Converts all cards in hand to a single random [[Card Suits|]] Available from start. ) has a similar effect but affects suit instead of rank and does not have a -1 hand-size downside.
