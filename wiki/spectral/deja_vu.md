# Deja Vu

Deja Vu is a Spectral that allows the player to set the seal on 1 selected card in hand to a Red Seal ().

## Usage notes

- Deja Vu can overwrite an existing seal on a playing card.

- Jokers that retrigger cards like Hack (Retrigger each played , , , ), Mime (), or Sock and Buskin () will only reactivate the card 1 additional time, and they will not reactivate the Seal a second time. For example, a played Red Seal King () will only activate 3 times, once for the initial trigger, once for the Red Seal, and then once more for Sock and Buskin.

- Red Seals work with both played scoring cards and held in hand cards.

- One of the easiest and most consistent ways to reach Balatro's de facto high score, naneinf, is to make as many Red Seal Steel ( ) Kings as possible. This is because with Baron (), a Red Seal on a Steel King essentially acts as 2 Steel Kings without a Red Seal. Because each time Baron triggers exponentially increases the score, the player needs to have hundreds of Baron triggers to reach naneinf. While Red Seals are not needed to achieve naneinf this way, it does make it substantially easier.

- Face Cards are the most versatile cards to have Red Seals on, even if they don't have any Enhancement or Edition. This is because they synergize with Photograph (), Triboulet (Played Kings and Queens each give when scored Find this [[Jokers|Joker]] from the [[Spectral Cards|]] card. ), Baron (), Shoot the Moon (Each held in hand gives Play every Heart card in your deck in one round. ), Smiley Face (), Scary Face (), Reserved Parking (), and Business Card ().

- Double or Nothing ( All cards become after scoring Standard 52-card deck, all with a Red Seal) starts with all cards with Red Seals.

## Synergies

### Decks

- Ghost Deck ( [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear in the shop, start with a card Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) makes finding the Deja Vu () card significantly easier.

### Jokers

Most Jokers benefit from Red Seals, especially those that trigger when certain cards are played. However, some Jokers benefit more from Red Seals than others, such as:

- Baron () will give X2.25 Mult for each Red Seal King held in hand, and ~X5.06 Mult if that King is a Steel Card ( ).

- Reserved Parking () will generally give $0.25 more on average.

 - This can change depending on retriggers and held in hand effects, see the Reserved Parking page for an explanation on the specific interactions and numbers.

- Bloodstone ( ), The Idol (Each played [[Card Ranks|]] of [[Card Suits|]] gives when scored<br><small>Card changes every round</small> In one hand, ), Ancient Joker (Each played card with [[Card Suits|]] gives when scored, <br> <small>suit changes at end of round</small> ), Photograph (), and Triboulet (Played Kings and Queens each give when scored Find this [[Jokers|Joker]] from the [[Spectral Cards|]] card. ) all benefit from Red Seals as they give exponentially more Mult the more times their effects are retriggered.

- Hiker () can also permanently add an additional +5 Chips, for a total of a permanent +10 Chips for Red Seal cards.

- Wee Joker (This Joker gains when each played is scored <br> }} ) can scale even faster with Red Seal 2 cards and gain +16 Chips, instead of the usual +8 Chips for each scored 2.

### Modifiers

- Steel Cards ( ) with a Red Seal give X2.25 Mult when held in hand.

- Gold Cards ( ) with a Red Seal give $6 if held at the end of the round.

 - With a large hand size, copy effects from Blueprint (Copies ability of [[Jokers|]] ) and/or Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ), retrigger effects from Mime (), and Red Seals, it's possible to generate thousands of dollars from this method.

- Glass Cards (<br> ) with a Red Seal give X4 Mult when played, and do not increase the chance of breaking.

- Polychrome Cards ( Edition Joker (Polychrome).png) with a Red Seal give X2.25 Mult when played.

## Trivia

- Déjà vu is a term used to describe a feeling in which one believes they have seen or experienced something already. The card's ability to apply a Red Seal to a playing card emphasizes this term by its ability to retrigger said card a second time.
