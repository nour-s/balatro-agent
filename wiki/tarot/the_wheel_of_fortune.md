# The Wheel of Fortune

, or Polychrome edition to a random Joker

The Wheel of Fortune is a Tarot that has a 1 in 4 chance to add an Edition to a random Joker.

## Usage notes

- The chosen edition is weighted at a 50% chance of making a Joker Foil ( Edition Joker (Foil).png), 35% chance of making a Joker Holographic ( Edition Joker (Holographic).png), and a 15% chance of making a Joker Polychrome ( Edition Joker (Polychrome).png).

 - When multiplying these with the default 25% chance, the combined odds are a 12.5% chance of making a Joker Foil ( Edition Joker (Foil).png), 8.75% chance of making a Joker Holographic ( Edition Joker (Holographic).png) and a 3.75% chance of making a Joker Polychrome ( Edition Joker (Polychrome).png).

- The number of jokers you have that Wheel could apply to does not change the probability of it working.

- The Wheel will not apply to any Joker that already has an edition (including Negative ( Joker or Consumable slot Edition Joker (Negative).png)). It also cannot be used if all Jokers already have an edition, and its Use button will be disabled.

- If The Wheel of Fortune misses the chance to apply an edition to a Joker, the card simply says the word, "Nope!"

## Trivia

- In certain bugged seeds (such as QV1LTNYQ and FAJ8UPYL), due to glitch in random number generation, The Wheel of Fortune can never trigger, unless with two Oops! All 6s (Doubles all [[listed probabilities| ]] <br> -> )}} In one hand, earn at least chips Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) Oops!).

- Its classic "Nope!" effect is getting a pin in a recently crowdfunded Pin Collection by Makeship along with Jimbo and a Cavendish ( <br> ) Joker.1]

 - Playstack also released a product where the "Nope!" effect appears on a bucket hat.2]

- The Wheel of Fortune has straightforward meanings like "good luck" and "a turning point" when faced upright, as well as reversed meanings of "bad luck" and "resistance to change". Both of these sets of meanings refer to the chanceful application of an edition to a random Joker, or rather a lack thereof.
