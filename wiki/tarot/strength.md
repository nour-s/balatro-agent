# Strength

Strength is a Tarot card that allows the player to increase the rank of two cards by one each. For example, a 4 would become a 5, a 10 would become a Jack, a King would become an Ace, and an Ace would become a 2.

## Usage notes

- To make many of the same rank for Poker Hands like Four of a Kind or Flush Five, or to make more of a desired rank for Jokers like Baron () or Photograph (), Strength is best used at the beginning, with Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) becoming far more powerful after a few cards have been Strengthened.

 - This is because, for example, while a 10 only requires 1 Strength card to turn into a Jack, each lower rank requires more Strength cards to make the same rank. Turning two 8s into a Jack requires 3 Strength cards, but only 2 Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) cards.

- When increasing the rank of face-down cards the cards will be briefly flipped. This can be used in a pinch to find out what a face-down card is.

- It should be noted that applying Strength on an Ace (a card rank that normally provides 11 Chips) will weaken it down to a card with a rank of 2 (which normally provides 2 Chips).

## Synergies

### Poker Hands

- Poker Hands that require 2 different ranks, like Two Pair and Full House, benefit the most from Strength, as there are more targets for it.

- Hands that require many of 1 rank like Four of a Kind and Flush Five also benefit from Strength, but have less targets for it.

- Straight benefits from consolidating lower ranks into higher ranks to make playing Straights more consistent, and from evening out the ranks if many of 1 Modified card was created through Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ). However, this does not give many targets for Strength.

### Jokers

Any Joker that is based around having a certain rank of card in the deck benefits from Strength, but some Jokers benefit more:

- Odd Todd (Played cards with [[Card Ranks|]] rank give when scored <br> ) and Even Steven (Played cards with [[Card Ranks|]] rank give when scored <br> ) affect every other rank of Aces and number cards, so Strength can be used on 20 different cards in a standard 52 deck to make them usable for these Jokers.

- Fibonacci (Each played , , , , ) immediately benefits from Kings, 4s, and 7s being Strengthened into Aces, 5s, and 8s, meaning that 12 different cards in a standard 52 card deck are good targets for Strength.

- Walkie Talkie () makes 3 and 9 good targets for Strength, making it slightly better than Jokers that only affect 1 rank.

## Trivia

- When faced upright, the Strength tarot card is meant to represent "inner strength", "courage", and "willpower", especially through compassion and patience rather than brute strength. This is heavily reflected through the in-game function of increasing the rank of any two cards, even if just by one rank at a time, as increasing the power of a card even slightly can make a huge difference in a run.
