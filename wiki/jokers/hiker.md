# Hiker

Copyable Perishable Eternal

Hiker is an Uncommon Chips Joker that permanently adds +5 Chips onto every played scoring card for the entirety of the run. The additional chips stack onto each other, so the next time a card is scored that has been boosted by Hiker, it will get an additional +5 Chips for a total of +10 Chips, etc. These bonus chips attached to the playing card cannot be removed and will be retained even if Hiker is sold, the card's suit/enhancement is changed, etc.

This also works with any Bonus Cards ( ) or Stone Cards (<br>); they will get an additional +5 Chips on top of the additional + Chips they already had. If a card with Hiker's additional chips was copied with Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ), Cryptid (Create copies of selected card in your hand Available from start. ) or DNA (If of round has only card, ), it will retain the bonuses from the copied card.

## Synergies

### Decks

- The Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) and Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) both have fewer cards than the standard 52 card deck, making finding the desired cards to level up with Hiker much easier. It also benefits from centralization around certain ranks, as those cards will already be played more often.

- The Red Deck ( ) allows for +1 discard to find cards more reliably.

- The Blue Deck ( hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) gives a +1 hand allowing the player to score more cards for Hiker.

- The Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) increases the player's hand size, making it easier to find desired cards, in addition to making better hands easier to find for those cards.

- The Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) can have certain configurations that make it easier to play hands that have 5 cards, allowing Hiker to level up more cards.

### Poker Hands

Hiker upgrades whenever cards are scored. Naturally, hands with more scoring cards will perform better with Hiker. In particular, Full House, Four of a Kind, Five of a Kind, Flush House, and Flush Five work well as they allow playing multiple of the same rank, upgrading multiple cards of that rank at the same time and making them more appealing and powerful to play in the future.

### Jokers

- Retrigger Jokers are invaluable to Hiker's success, as they allow cards to be scored multiple times and get upgraded multiple times. In addition, each scoring will add multitudes of chips to the final score, as the cards played ideally have dozens, even hundreds of bonus chips.

 - Sock and Buskin () and Hack (Retrigger each played , , , ) work best for these purposes, as their retriggers are only rank-reliant.

- Splash (Every counts in scoring Common Effect N/A (Passive) ) has an interesting synergy with Hiker. Because it scores all cards played, certain cards can be upgraded with Hiker that normally would not be due to the hand. This dual combo can act as a strong chip provider for High Card and Pair builds, as one can focus on playing the same cards again and again to continuously upgrade them without caring about rank.

- Jokers that give Mult based on scored cards are helpful, such as Smiley Face () and Fibonacci (Each played , , , , ), especially if they also benefit from the retriggers Hiker loves.

- Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ) can work well with Hiker, as it requires many hands to be played to work the best. A number of other Jokers like this benefit as well, like Ride the Bus (This Joker gains per hand played without a scoring card <br> }} ) and Spare Trousers (This Joker gains if played hand contains a [[Poker Hands|]] <br> }} ).

- Vampire (This Joker gains per scoring played, removes card <br> }} ) can occasionally work well with Hiker. The bonus chips Hiker provides makes up for the lack of enhancements on cards. This idea also combos well with Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ), as one can end up playing many hands to get as much value from Vampire and Vagabond as possible while also upgrading all their cards. This does weaken one of Hiker's main draws, though, that being buffing already powerful modified cards to even greater heights.

- DNA (If of round has only card, ), ironically, works best with Hiker after Hiker has been sold. Creating copies of cards will usually end up flooding the deck with too many cards to upgrade in a round, and takes away a hand that could otherwise be used upgrading cards. It is better to copy an already powerful card after Hiker is sold to retroactively make it as if Hiker buffed an additional card each round it was used.

- Wee Joker (This Joker gains when each played is scored <br> }} ) needs 2s to be played to upgrade it, however, 2s are weak early game due to their low base chip count. Hiker can buff a player's 2s to 12 Chips from just 2 triggers, which is higher than a base face card value of 10 Chips.

### Seals

- Red Seals () can also retrigger cards for Hiker, giving an additional permanent +5 Chips, for a total of +10 Chips, each time the card is played.

### Vouchers

- Wasteful () and Recyclomancy () both give +1 discard, which helps the player to find cards more reliably.

- Grabber () and Nacho Tong (Permanently gain an additional hand per round Play a total of 2,) both give +1 hand, allowing the player to score more cards for Hiker.

- Paint Brush ( ) and Palette ( hand size Reduce your hand size down to five cards. ) both increase the player's hand size, making it easier to find desired cards, in addition to making better hands easier to find for those cards.

### Poker Hands

- High Card and Pair are generally the worst to play with Hiker, as the fewest cards are scored. There are some circumstances where they are decent, but those circumstances are uncommon. High Card and Pair have the least amount of scored playing cards, making it less useful with Hiker since only those cards will be enhanced. However, it is possible to make these Poker Hands work if the circumstances are right (ex. Splash (Every counts in scoring Common Effect N/A (Passive) )).

## Trivia

- This Joker's name and art is a pun on the word "hike", as it can also mean a sudden price increase, similar to how its effect increases a playing card's Chips.

- Hiker is the only Joker that can permanently and repetitively buff playing cards.
