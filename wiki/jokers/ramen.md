# Ramen

Mult per card discarded Copyable Perishable Eternal

Ramen is an Uncommon Joker that provides Multiplicative Mult to all hands played. When first acquired, it serves as a middling but unconditional X2 Mult. However, as luck favors the user less and the rounds drag on, this Joker decreases in power, decreasing by X0.01 Mult per card discarded. Ramen cannot maintain or go below X1 Mult; once 100 cards are discarded, it will automatically destroy itself, with the text "Eaten!" flashing on the screen when it does.

The Mult decrease triggers both on the player's per-round discards and cards discarded by other sources - the latter currently only being The Hook ( ). Ramen will not trigger if the played hand in a round contains a card(s) that did not score, as they are not considered to have been discarded. This Joker's sell value is also never decreased regardless of its current Mult value, so it is advised to sell it once it reaches or goes below around X1.25 Mult, at which point its scoring assistance will be minimal.

Ramen has extremely limited utility past Ante 8 due to its middling base power and reverse power scaling. However, in the absence of another Multiplicative Mult Joker, it can still serve most builds very well.

## Synergies

### Decks

- Green Deck ( At end of each Round: <br> per remaining <br> per remaining <br> Earn no Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ): Perhaps the most obvious synergy exists with this Deck; conserving discards for money gives a mild source of income to compensate for the extra hands required to capitalize on Ramen's power.

- Blue Deck ( hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ): The extra hand every round makes it less punishing to play 'throwaway' hands as makeshift discards that also ease a bit of scoring tension, which better preserves this Joker's power.

- Nebula Deck ( Start run with the voucher <br> consumable slot Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ): With Ramen skewed more towards easier-to-find hands in desperate need of scaling, the built-in Telescope ([[Booster Packs|]] always contain the [[Planet Cards|]] card for your most played [[Poker Hands|]] ) Voucher this Deck provides makes it a natural choice.

- All Decks with consistency-based bonuses allow them to form better hands in the absence of discards, which helps make much more out of Ramen than usual. These include the Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) (+2 hand size), the Checkered Deck ( ) (only  Hearts and  Spades), and especially the Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) (no face cards), which can very easily leverage the fast scaling of Straights with the Saturn (Increases hand value by and Available from start. ) Planet Card in tandem with this Joker. Even certain Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) seeds can lend themselves well to consistently finding high-performing hands.

### Poker Hands

This Joker works best with weaker hands that can be more reliably made without discarding (or by using a hand as a makeshift discard), such as High Card, Pair, Two Pair, and Three of a Kind. To that end, this will force the player to become more dependent on Planet Card scaling than with most other builds, as all these hands are very weak at a baseline. Three of a Kinds should be prioritized if possible, as they have slightly more base + Mult and gain twice as much through Venus (Increases hand value by and Available from start. ) as the others do ( Pluto (Increases hand value by and Available from start. ), Mercury (Increases hand value by and Available from start. ), and Uranus (Increases hand value by and Available from start. ), respectively).

### Jokers

This Joker is the only Multiplicative Mult Joker that objectively gets weaker over time. On top of its already average X2 Mult multiplier, it is best used in the earlier Antes as a 'transitional' Joker to be used while saving up money to gain more dependable sources of scoring.

The following Jokers synergize well with Ramen:

- Any Common Joker that can provide consistent shots of + Mult will benefit a build with this Joker greatly, allowing rounds to be solved faster, and as a result, more money to be conserved for longer-term options. Some of the better options include Abstract Joker ( for each [[Jokers|]] card<br>}} ) (which is viable even in the later Antes), Popcorn ( <br> ) (itself a temporary addition), and Half Joker ( ), Scholar (), and Walkie Talkie () (which are all Jokers that can easily be built around while still being strong).

- Banner ( ): Providing +30 Chips to all hands played for each unused discard, these two can cover both sides of the equation with almost any hand in the early game. The -1 discard malus inflicted by Blue Stake and higher makes this a less viable combo in the later difficulties, but it is still worth considering.

- Blue Joker ( for each remaining card in <br> }} ): Providing +2 Chips to all hands played for each card remaining in the deck, this essentially beats out Banner ( ) on the higher Stakes and can go even higher by adding cards to the deck, albeit this is ill-advised in combination with this Joker due to an absolute need for consistency.

- Delayed Gratification (Earn per if no discards are used by end of the round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ): This economy Joker rewards the user with $2 for each discard the player has at the end of the round, as long as no discards were used during the round. This can much more quickly help the player access more build stability when combined with the unique property of this Joker. Much like Banner ( ), however, it is far less viable on Blue Stake or higher, as it essentially becomes a Golden Joker (Earn at end of round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ) with an inconvenient activation condition.

- Green Joker ( per hand played <br> per discard <br> }} ): A steady-scaling Additive Mult Joker that gains +1 Mult for every hand played but loses -1 Mult for every discard action (including The Hook). This is the only other anti-discard Joker that ignores the number of remaining discards, making it far more effective on all Stakes. Adding onto this, its Additive Mult pairs extremely well with the Multiplicative Mult this Joker gives, allowing them to comprise a respectable amount of your score alone, even into the late game!

- Burglar (When [[Blind|]] is selected, ): An amazing partner with this Joker, replacing the player's discards with 3 extra hands at the start of the round. This effectively turns Ramen into an X2 Mult with no drawbacks, which, with enough Additive Mult, can serve well even into Ante 8 when it otherwise would not. It also enables synergy with Mystic Summit ( ), which normally has anti-synergy with this Joker, giving a respectable shot of +15 Mult to more easily clear the Blinds. However, it does turn off synergy with Banner ( ) and Delayed Gratification (Earn per if no discards are used by end of the round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ), so consider other options if these are key to the current build.

- Astronomer (All cards and in the shop are Discover every card Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): One of the few effect Jokers that Ramen has synergy with, making Planet Cards and Celestial Packs in The Shop free. In doing so, it becomes easier to lean on poker hand level scaling in the event that scoring Jokers prove too inaccessible due to bad luck.

- Space Joker ( chance to upgrade level of played [[Poker Hands|]] ): With each played hand having a 1 in 4 chance to have its level upgraded before scoring, it can serve as a source of long-term scaling, especially while playing the large number of hands per round this Joker encourages.

### Vouchers

- Grabber () and its upgraded counterpart, Nacho Tong (Permanently gain an additional hand per round Play a total of 2,), provide an extra hand with each acquisition, giving more opportunities to find higher-scoring hands to play.

- Telescope ([[Booster Packs|]] always contain the [[Planet Cards|]] card for your most played [[Poker Hands|]] ) guarantees that the Planet Card for the most played poker hand will always appear in Celestial Packs. Due to the very low base Mult of High Card, Pair, and Two Pair, this is a good addition to any build keeping Ramen for the long term.

- For similar reasons, Planet Merchant ( ) and Tarot Merchant ( ), as well as their upgraded counterparts Planet Tycoon ([[Planet Cards|]] ) and Tarot Tycoon ( ), are also possible considerations, albeit not as strongly recommended since they take notable Shop space away from Jokers, which are usually the main source of scoring.

### Blinds

- The Water ( ) will always set your discards to 0, making it impossible to decrease Ramen.

- Amber Acorn ( ): If there are too many Jokers to keep track of otherwise, a single-card discard will trigger Ramen, revealing its location.

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Red Deck ( ): This Deck has anti-synergy with this Joker, as it encourages the player to discard more to find better hands.

- Black Deck ( Joker slot <br> hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) makes it very difficult to use Ramen, as it suffers from -1 hand per round. This forces more dependency on discards while disallowing liberal use of 'throwaway' hands.

- Similarly, Blast Off ( hands per round<br> discards per round<br> Joker Slots Eternal <br>Eternal <br><br>Banned [[Vouchers]]: , <br>Banned Jokers: ) makes it even more difficult to use Ramen, as this deck only has 2 hands. This forces much more dependency on discards while disallowing liberal use of 'throwaway' hands.

### Jokers

- Drunkard ( discard each round Common Effect N/A (Passive) ) and Merry Andy ( discards each round, <br> hand size Win a run in or fewer rounds Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): The extra discards these two provide are almost useless in a Ramen build, except when considering the potential presence of Banner ( ) or Delayed Gratification (Earn per if no discards are used by end of the round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ). Using 3 Joker slots for such a combination can badly dampen scoring, so these two discard giving Jokers should generally be avoided, specially Merry Andy ( discards each round, <br> hand size Win a run in or fewer rounds Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ) as it decreases hand size.

- Faceless Joker (), Mail-In Rebate (Earn for each discarded [[Card Ranks|]], ), and Trading Card (If of round has only card, ): These Jokers cannot give their payout unless Ramen gets decreased. Money does lead to better scoring, but the risk of depreciating Mult even a little is huge.

- Troubadour ( hand size, <br> hand each round Win consecutive rounds by <abbr title="Discards are OK to use.">playing only 1 hand</abbr> Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): While its hand size bonus provides opportunity to more consistently find hands with higher base Mult, it has a debilitating -1 hand per round penalty, meaning it forces more dependency on discards that would weaken this Joker.

- Burnt Joker (Upgrade the level of the first poker hand each round Sell 50 cards. ): Though a source of much-needed level scaling, it does this through discarding the poker hand in question, which actively worsens Ramen.

- Castle (This Joker gains per discarded [[Card Suits|]] card, suit changes every round <br> }} ) cannot gain chips if the player doesn't discard.

- Hit the Road (This Joker gains for every discarded this round<br> }} Discard at the same time. ) and Yorick (This Joker gains every cards discarded<br>}} Find this [[Jokers|Joker]] from the [[Spectral Cards|]] card. ): The power of these cards both depends on discarding large quantities of cards, meaning they will both greedily slurp your Ramen right up. Yorick is usually worth keeping over this Joker, however, as it has the potential to reach very high XMult values if acquired early.

- Mystic Summit ( ) has anti-synergy with Ramen unless the player has Burglar (When [[Blind|]] is selected, ), as it wants to get rid of all discards and therefore weaken Ramen.

### Card Modifiers

- A card with a Purple Seal (Creates a card when <br> ) needs to be discarded in order to get value from it, which lowers Ramen's Mult.

### Vouchers

- Wasteful () and Recyclomancy (): the extra Discards provided by these two vouchers are almost useless in a Ramen build, except when considering the potential presence of Banner ( ) or Delayed Gratification (Earn per if no discards are used by end of the round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ).

- Hieroglyph ( Ante, <br> ) should be avoided while using this Joker, as its -1 hand per round penalty increases dependence on discards.

### Blinds

- The Hook ( ) will cause Ramen to lose X0.02 Mult per hand played, unless hand size is small enough to leave fewer than 2 cards in hand after playing.

## Trivia

- This Joker is part of the group of "Consumable" Jokers that can be destroyed with usage, dubbed "Food Jokers" due to their shared theme. The other Jokers in this group are Ice Cream (<br> ), Popcorn ( <br> ), Gros Michel (<br> ), Cavendish ( <br> ), Egg (Gains of at end of round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ), Seltzer (), Turtle Bean ( hand size, reduces by each round Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ), and Diet Cola (Sell this card to create a free [[Tags|]] Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ).1]2]3]

 - Of the five Food Jokers that scale down, Ramen is the only Joker that can avoid being scaled down throughout a run. The others are scaled down after hands are played or rounds are passed, both of which are necessary for progress.
