# Triboulet

Copyable Perishable Eternal

Triboulet is one of the five Legendary Jokers that can be generated from using The Soul (Creates a [[Legendary Jokers|]] Joker <br> Available from start. ) Spectral card, which in itself has a 3 in 1000 (0.3%) chance to replace any given card in a Spectral Pack or Arcana Pack.

Triboulet gives X2 Mult whenever a King or Queen is scored.

## Synergies

### Decks

Most decks benefit equally from Triboulet as most start with the same amount of Kings and Queen.

- An Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) seed containing high numbers of Kings and Queens will allow this Joker to reach a much higher ceiling much easier.

- The Ghost Deck ( [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear in the shop, start with a card Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) has easier access to Spectral cards, which simplifies deck building, allowing the deck to be more concentrated on Kings and Queens, but also because of Red Seals (), which can retrigger Triboulet's effect.

### Poker Hands

- To make the most value out of Triboulet, bigger poker hands are preferred, such as Full House, Flush House, Five of a Kind or Flush Five. This also means that, inversely, smaller poker hands like High Card or Pair get less triggers of Triboulet, and therefore are not recommended.

### Jokers

- As Kings and Queens are considered face cards, any Jokers triggering off of face cards naturally make a combo with Triboulet. Retriggers are by far the most valuable, as seen with Sock and Buskin (), activating the multiplicative Mult up to 5 times more. This skyrockets gain from Triboulet when playing a 5-card hand containing only scoring Kings and Queens which is X32 Mult to X1024 Mult, a staggering difference.

- Hanging Chad (), Dusk (), and Seltzer () are also good due to the same reasoning.

- Splash (Every counts in scoring Common Effect N/A (Passive) ) allows Kings and Queens to score on poker hands they otherwise would be unable to, such as an Ace-high High Card hand.

### Consumables

- Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) can turn some cards into powerful Queens or Kings.

- The Hanged Man (Destroys up to selected cards Available from start. ) can remove non-Queen and non-King cards.

- Strength (Increases rank of up to selected cards by Available from start. ) can turn Jacks into Queens or even turn Queens into Kings depending on the build.

- Cryptid (Create copies of selected card in your hand Available from start. ) can make more Queens or Kings.

- Deja Vu (Add a [[Red Seal|]] to selected card in your hand Available from start. ) can apply Red Seals onto Queens or Kings, allowing for more Triboulet triggers.

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ), Medusa ( Eternal 52 cards, with all face cards replaced by Stone cards), and Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) are not recommended due to the lack of initial Kings and Queens.

### Jokers

- Canio (This Joker gains when a card is destroyed <br> }} Find this [[Jokers|Joker]] from the [[Spectral Cards|]] card. ) requires many face cards to be destroyed, the majority of which tend to be Kings and Queens. This isn't too much of a problem if the player decides to destroy just Jacks or if they have Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ).

- Baron (), Shoot the Moon (Each held in hand gives Play every Heart card in your deck in one round. ) and Reserved Parking () require having Kings and Queens held in hand instead of playing them, making a bad combination with Triboulet.

 - Though Reserved Parking () isn't too much of an issue if the player leaves just Jacks or have Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ).

### Boss Blinds

- The Plant ( ) can be a run killer as debuffed Queens and Kings do not trigger Triboulet.

## Trivia

- Little biographical information is available about the jester Triboulet. It is known that in France, there have been at least three jesters referred to as "Triboulet", with the first one serving René of Anjou and also being a playwright, the second serving Louis XII, and the third one serving Francis I (and possibly also Louis XII). The second Triboulet died during Louis XII's reign, and he and the Third Triboulet later became confused as one person.

- The effect of this Joker is likely in reference to a story in which a court jester slapped the King on the buttocks. As the tale goes, the King told the jester he would be allowed to live if he could give an answer more offensive than the act itself. In response, the jester said, “I’m sorry, your majesty- I mistook you for the Queen!” This would explain why the effect of the joker specifically applies to only Kings and Queens. Although the historical accuracy of this story and quote is questionable, it is commonly attributed to Triboulet.

- This Joker is animated, with Triboulet hovering just above the blue card face.
