# Blueprint

Copyable Perishable Eternal

Blueprint is a Rare Effect Joker that copies the effect of the Joker to its immediate right. Not all Jokers are compatible with Blueprint, as Blueprint cannot copy any "passive modifier" effects, but Blueprint itself is compatible. Blueprint's compatibility with other Jokers can be seen under its effect. This Joker is unlocked once the player wins their first run.

In general, Blueprint will not copy the following effects:

- Anything that triggers at the end of a round (ex. Golden Joker (Earn at end of round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ))

- Debuffed Jokers

- Card Edition effects

- Sticker effects

- Card destruction (ex. Trading Card (If of round has only card, ))

- Joker scaling (only the final result is copied)

- Changes to hands, discards, hand size or hand typing (except for Burglar (When [[Blind|]] is selected, ), which triggers after the blind starts)

- Passive effects (like Chicot (Disables effect of every [[Blinds|]] Find this [[Jokers|Joker]] from the [[Spectral Cards|]] card. ) or Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ))

See Incompatible Jokers for a full list.

## Synergies

### Decks

By its nature, any deck can make effective use of Blueprint. Its effect is highly flexible and can benefit nearly any archetype of deck, aside from Madness (When or is selected, gain and a random Joker <br> }} ) without Eternal () Stickers.

### Poker Hands

Any hand can benefit from Blueprint in the usual ways. The only hand-specific Jokers that are incompatible with Blueprint are independent effects such as Shortcut (Allows to be made with gaps of <br> )}} Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ).

### Jokers

Blueprint is an essential Joker and combines well with virtually any other Joker to double its effect.

- When stacked with Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ) or another Blueprint (), each will add one more copy of the copied effect.

 - When combining Blueprint with Brainstorm, Blueprint can be placed to the left of Brainstorm or to the left of the leftmost Joker (to be copied), depending on the desired order of Joker activation.

- As a rule of thumb, copying a Joker with X Mult or retrigger is usually better than copying +Chips or +Mult. The optimal placement for maximum score can be difficult to determine, so consider all possible options.

- When multiple copies are combined with Baron () and Mime (), while using Plasma Deck (), players can achieve the elusive Naneinf score.

- Copying a Joker that earns $money or creates cards (especially Tarot) is a great option when boosting score is not necessary.

- When copying chance-based Jokers like Space Joker ( chance to upgrade level of played [[Poker Hands|]] ), the chance is rolled independently for each copy of that Joker. This means if one Joker triggers, the copies are not guaranteed to trigger at the same time.

- A few Jokers have both "trigger" effects that Blueprint can copy and "passive" ones that it cannot. Stuntman (,<br> hand size Earn at least 100 million (100,000,000) Chips in a single hand. ), for example, gives +250 Chips, a "trigger" effect, and -2 Hand Size, a "passive" effect. Therefore, Blueprint copies the +250 Chips, but not the -2 Hand Size.

- If jokers that take effect upon selling them ( Luchador (Sell this card to disable the current [[Blinds|]] Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ) and Diet Cola (Sell this card to create a free [[Tags|]] Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ), not including Invisible Joker (After rounds, sell this card to a random Joker <br> /2}} <br> ]] from copy)}} Win a run without ever having more than Rare Effect N/A (Passive) )) get copied, Blueprint can be sold to trigger the effect.

 - However, on mobile devices, dragging Blueprint to sell it could change the order of Jokers, changing its effect.

- Moving Blueprint can often maximize its potential by always copying the most important Joker for the next action taken. Once a Poker hand is played, Blueprint will continue copying the last Joker to its right, even if it is moved around afterward. For example, one round could involve copying:

 - Perkeo (Creates a [[Negative|]] copy of random [[Consumables|]] card in your possession at the end of the [[The Shop|]] Find this [[Jokers|Joker]] from the [[Spectral Cards|]] card. Legendary Effect N/A (Passive) ) when exiting the shop

 - Cartomancer (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] card when [[Blinds|]] is selected <br> Discover every Tarot Card. ) or Burglar (When [[Blind|]] is selected, ) when starting the Blind

 - Burnt Joker (Upgrade the level of the first poker hand each round Sell 50 cards. ) during the first discard

 - Business Card () during the following hands while playing face cards

 - Mail-In Rebate (Earn for each discarded [[Card Ranks|]], ) during the following discards

### Blinds

- Amber Acorn ( ): This boss blind flips and shuffles Jokers around, which can make Blueprint copy the incorrect Joker, or copy nothing at all if it happens to be moved to the rightmost position or to the left of an incompatible Joker, for at least one hand.

## Notes

It should be noted that if the player presses "Play Hand", then quickly attempts to move Blueprint to a different position in the joker panel while the hand is scoring, the Blueprint will copy the effect of the joker that it was to the left of before the hand is played. For example, if a player has three jokers, positioned in this way before playing this hand: Blueprint (), Greedy Joker (), and Fibonacci (Each played , , , , ), then attempted to move the Blueprint to the right of Greedy Joker and to the left of Fibonacci immediately after the "Play Hand" button is triggered, the Blueprint will trigger the effect of Greedy Joker instead of Fibonacci.

However, it is also worth noting that the copy order reapplies once you beat the round. For example, if you move your Blueprint to copy Mime () while your winning hand is scoring, then you will receive the benefits of multiple copies of Mime during the end-of-round effects (such as Gold Cards ( ) or Blue Seal (Creates the card for final played [[Poker Hands|]] of round if in hand<br> )). Players should move all of their copies of Blueprint () and Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ) onto Mime while their final hand is scoring for maximum Gold Card and Blue Seal value.

## Trivia

- Certain visuals upon triggering a Joker effect, like the "Level Up!" from a copied Space Joker ( chance to upgrade level of played [[Poker Hands|]] ), have a blue background instead of the standard orange one.

- At $10 each, Blueprint and its sibling Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ) are the most expensive Jokers able to be purchased. (Legendary Jokers would cost $20 if they were ever purchasable.)

- The card art depicts a deliberate, professionally-produced blueprint. This is in contrast with the rough 'back of the napkin' sketching of Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ).

- The background art used to span the whole card pre-release.

- The Joker in the art is looking to its left (the right of the card), at the Joker it is copying.

- Blueprint is one of the four jokers that is featured in the official physical playing card deck. The 3 others are Joker ( ), Juggler ( hand size Common Effect N/A (Passive) ), and Gros Michel (<br> ).
