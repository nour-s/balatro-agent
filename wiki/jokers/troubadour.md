# Troubadour

Copyable Perishable Eternal

Troubadour is an Uncommon Effect Joker that provides +2 hand size at the cost of 1 Hand being taken away when Blind is selected.

To unlock this Joker, the player must win 5 consecutive rounds by playing only 1 hand.

## Usage Notes

- The Five-Card Draw ( discards per round<br> hand size<br> Joker slots <br>Banned Jokers: , , ) challenge bans Troubadour.

- Because the hand is taken away when Blind is selected, gaining Troubadour during a round will not reduce the amount of hands until the next Blind, and selling Troubadour during a blind will only remove the extra Hand Size while keeping the -1 hand effect for that Blind.

- Troubadour can never decrease the total amount of hands below 1, even with multiple copies or when used during The Needle ( ) or Golden Needle ( Discards each cost <br><br> hands per round<br> discards per round<br>Start with Banned Jokers: <br>Banned [[Vouchers]]: , ).

## Synergies

### Decks

- Blue Deck ( hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) works best with Troubadour thanks to its +1 hand per round bonus, offsetting the -1 hand penalty of this Joker. This effectively transforms the Blue Deck into a more flexible Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ).

- Golden Needle ( Discards each cost <br><br> hands per round<br> discards per round<br>Start with Banned Jokers: <br>Banned [[Vouchers]]: , ) starts with 1 hand, and Troubadour cannot reduce hands below one, turning Troubadour into a +2 hand size without any penalty.

### Poker Hands

Any strong 5 card hands can benefit from Troubadour, as the increased hand size means a higher chance to draw needed cards to form the hand.

Troubadour can also be good in High-card builds that rely on cards being in hand to score, such as with Baron () or Steel cards.

### Jokers

- Any held-in-hand effect Joker will be improved by Troubadour by virtue of holding more cards in hand.

 - This includes Baron (), as mentioned previously, Shoot the Moon (Each held in hand gives Play every Heart card in your deck in one round. ), and Reserved Parking ().

- Mime (): More cards in hand means more cards for Mime to retrigger.

- Mail-In Rebate (Earn for each discarded [[Card Ranks|]], ): With increased hand size, it can be easier to find the card rank required for that round.

- Burglar (When [[Blind|]] is selected, ): Add three extra hands, offsetting the -1 hand penalty of this Joker at the cost of no discards.

- Baseball Card ([[Uncommon Jokers|]] ): As Troubadour itself does not directly increase score, having Baseball Card would give it immediate scoring value. It may be ideal to pair these two together due to the -1 hand penalty, which may make defeating Blinds more difficult.

### Card Modifiers

With Troubadour's increased hand size, it can make it easier to draw into Steel Cards ( ) and/or Gold Cards ( ), as well as cards that have a Blue Seal (Creates the card for final played [[Poker Hands|]] of round if in hand<br> ).

### Vouchers

- Grabber () and Nacho Tong (Permanently gain an additional hand per round Play a total of 2,) can mitigate Troubadour's -1 hand penalty.

### Blinds

- With The Needle ( ), it effectively provides +2 hand size without penalty, as players always start with 1 hand per round.

- If Crimson Heart ( ) disables Troubadour, the hand size will return to normal but the current hand will stay intact until the next discard (ex: 10/8). By quitting to the Main Menu and returning, the hand size actually permanently increases (ex: 10/10), giving players a permanent hand size upgrade.

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Black Deck ( Joker slot <br> hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) makes it difficult to use Troubadour on, as its -1 hand per round penalty means players will only have 2 hands to beat each blind.

 - Similarly, Blast Off ( hands per round<br> discards per round<br> Joker Slots Eternal <br>Eternal <br><br>Banned [[Vouchers]]: , <br>Banned Jokers: ) makes it even more difficult to use Troubadour on, as its -1 hand per round penalty means players will only have 1 hand to beat each blind.

### Jokers

- Overall, any scaling Joker that requires hands to be played is impeded by the -1 hand penalty, giving fewer opportunities to scale them efficiently.

 - This includes Green Joker ( per hand played <br> per discard <br> }} ), Ride the Bus (This Joker gains per hand played without a scoring card <br> }} ), Supernova (Adds the number of times [[Poker Hands|]] ), Runner (Gains if played hand contains a <br> }} ), Square Joker (This Joker gains if played hand has exactly cards <br> }} ), Spare Trousers (This Joker gains if played hand contains a [[Poker Hands|]] <br> }} ), and Obelisk (This Joker gains per hand played without playing your most played <br> }} ).

- Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ) and Space Joker ( chance to upgrade level of played [[Poker Hands|]] ) suffer from the -1 hand penalty, as Jokers that generate a resource or trigger when a hand is played have fewer opportunities to trigger.

- Card Sharp ( ) can activate one less time.

- Loyalty Card ( every hands played<br> ) becomes harder to activate.

- Blackboard ( ) can be harder to trigger, as the increased hand size can make it harder to hold only  Clubs and  Spades in your hand.

- More cards in hand mean that Blue Joker ( for each remaining card in <br> }} ) will give fewer chips.

- The increased hand size will make it hard for Raised Fist () to give more score from high-ranked cards.

### Tags

- Handy Tag ( ) will give less money.

### Vouchers

- Hieroglyph ( Ante, <br> ), as it gives an additional -1 hand, should be avoided if Troubadour is essential to the current Joker build. Coupled with the Black Deck ( Joker slot <br> hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ), it can reduce the total number of hands to as low as 1.

### Blinds

- The House ( ) is particularly bad for big hand size, as it means that more cards will be drawn face down.

## Trivia

- A troubadour was someone who composed music and performed poetry during the Middle Ages. Troubadour songs were mainly about chivalry and courtly love.
