# Cloud 9

in your full deck at end of round
 (Currently $4) Copyable Perishable Eternal
Cloud 9 is an Uncommon Economy Joker. It pays out an additional $1 at the end of every round for each 9 in the player's complete Deck - regardless of whether they were scored, drawn, or discarded that round.

Assuming no Deck modification, or skewing by an Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) seed, it will generate $4 every round - which makes it just as effective as the Common Golden Joker (Earn at end of round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ), but slightly worse to start as it costs a dollar extra. However, with the right amount of Deck manipulation, this Joker can earn substantial amounts of money, especially when playing stronger hands like Four of a Kind and Five of a Kind to save on hands each round.

## Synergies

### Decks

Any Deck can use Cloud 9 effectively, as it pays out whenever a round is completed as long as the player has at least one 9 in their full Deck.

- Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ): Since it can benefit greatly from Cloud 9 if the seed happens to include a higher number of 9s, this is the only Deck that inherently synergizes with it.

- Black Deck ( Joker slot <br> hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) can benefit from Cloud 9 thanks to its +1 Joker Slot. Because of the -1 hand per blind penalty, Cloud 9 can help offset the lower income from early games.

- Green Deck ( At end of each Round: <br> per remaining <br> per remaining <br> Earn no Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ): This Deck likes to have extra income sources in the absence of interest, so something like Cloud 9 is a natural inclusion.

- Yellow Deck ( Start with extra Discover at least items from your [[Collection|collection]] ): This Joker will help maintain the interest cap when acquired in the mid-game, further increasing the already-strong buying power it has.

- Ghost Deck ( [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear in the shop, start with a card Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ), Magic Deck ( Start run with the voucher and copies of Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ), and Zodiac Deck ( Start run with , , and Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ): All these Decks have easier access to Deck fixing to include a higher amount of 9s in the Deck. Of these, Ghost Deck is probably the best to choose, as its starting Hex (Add [[Card Modifiers#Editions|]] to a random [[Jokers|]], destroy all other Jokers Available from start. ) Spectral Card makes it more practical to keep this Joker for the long run by allowing it to help with scoring.

- Odd Todd (Played cards with [[Card Ranks|]] rank give when scored <br> ): With its extra shot of Chips whenever an odd-ranked card is scored, it can dish out up to an extremely high +155 Chips boost when five 9s are scored in the same hand (and even more with retrigger effects).

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ), Blast Off ( hands per round<br> discards per round<br> Joker Slots Eternal <br>Eternal <br><br>Banned [[Vouchers]]: , <br>Banned Jokers: ) and Cruelty ( give no reward money<br> give no reward money<br><br> Joker Slots ): The reduced Joker slots makes it difficult to carry Cloud 9 since players may need more scoring Jokers to survive and beat Ante 8.

- Anaglyph Deck ( After defeating each [[Blinds|]], gain a [[File:|24px]] [[Tags|]] Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ): As the player ideally wants to avoid skipping to make use of Cloud 9's payout at the end of the round, this Deck has a slight anti-synergy with it.

### Jokers

- Showman ([[Jokers|]], [[Tarot Cards|]], [[Planet Cards|]], and [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear multiple times Reach Ante level Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): This Joker can make it harder to find good Deck-fixing cards in The Shop, as well as Arcana and Spectral Packs, by allowing duplicates to appear, meaning that holding onto less valuable consumables will not prevent them from reappearing.

- Throwback ( for each [[Blinds|]] skipped this run <br> }} ): Skipping Blinds is less than ideal for most Economy Jokers, to which Cloud 9 is no exception.

## Trivia

- This is the only Economy Joker in the game without a true cap on its money generation - most of the others have a fixed amount (like Golden Joker (Earn at end of round Common Economy N/A (Passive) )), are limited by the number of hands/discards per round (like Faceless Joker () and To Do List (Earn if [[Poker Hands|]] is a [[Poker Hands|]], )), or have a hard cap by some other means (like Satellite (Earn at end of round per unique card used this run Have at least . Uncommon Economy N/A (Passive) ) and To the Moon (Earn an extra of for every you have at end of round Uncommon Economy N/A (Passive) )). As long as the player has a large number of 9s, Cloud 9 will continue to pay out $1 for each.

- There are exactly nine clouds on the card art for this Joker.

- The name of this Joker is possibly a reference to the phrase 'on cloud 9', referring to being in a state of euphoria.
