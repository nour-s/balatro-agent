# Wee Joker

(Currently +0 Chips) Copyable Perishable Eternal

Wee Joker is a Rare scaling Chips Joker that adds Chips to played hands. The amount of Chips added begins at +0 and is permanently increased by +8 whenever a 2 is scored. This also works with retriggers, such as the likes of Hack (Retrigger each played , , , ) or Red Seal (). It will not gain Chips if the played 2 is not scored.

Wee Joker is one of, if not the, strongest Chips Joker there is to offer thanks to its absurd scaling capabilities. Once the player can score 2s consistently, Wee Joker shines as a steady Chips source that can reach Stuntman (,<br> hand size Earn at least 100 million (100,000,000) Chips in a single hand. ) levels of potency quite quickly and also has the potential to take off into the 4 digits, with some highly proficient scaling reaching 10s of thousands of Chips. To do so, poker hands that use a high number of playing cards are basically required, such as Four of a Kind, Five of a Kind or Flush Five.

Taking Wee Joker late into the run, when deck-fixing is already in the works, may still be worthwhile in case the player severely lacks a source of Chips. The player may have removed 2s beforehand though, which can make Wee Joker way more difficult to use. Instead, it is advised that the player first remove 7s and 8s, since their synergies are often the least impactful. Doing this on a Straight build is a no-go, however.

To unlock this Joker, the player must win a run in 18 rounds or less.

## Synergies

### Decks

Wee Joker synergizes well with all Decks fairly equally, but some have minor advantages over the others.

Most decks have the same starting amount of four 2s, making Wee Joker synergize with them fairly equally. However, some decks have features that may make utilizing Wee Joker easier:

- Red Deck ( ): The extra Discard allows the player to cycle through more cards, which helps with finding and scoring 2s greatly.

- Blue Deck ( hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ): The extra Hand lets the player use a "throwaway" hand for extra scaling, and also acts as a pseudo-Discard, again helping with finding and scoring 2s.

- Magic Deck ( Start run with the voucher and copies of Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ): With the help of the two starting copies of The Fool (Creates the last [[Tarot Cards|]] or [[Planet Cards|]] card used during this run<br><small> excluded</small> Available from start. ), the player can potentially create a deck centralized around 2s right at the very beginning. This can help with playing into Wee Joker later tremendously, provided the player can find the Joker. Try copying a Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) to convert cards into 2s, or The Hanged Man (Destroys up to selected cards Available from start. ) to get rid of many non-2s at once.

- Ghost Deck ( [[Spectral Cards|]] cards may appear in the shop, start with a card Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ): Thanks to its easier access to Spectral Cards, the Ghost Deck has great potential to manipulate the deck into being beneficial to Wee Joker. Try looking for Cryptid (Create copies of selected card in your hand Available from start. ) to create multiple new 2s, or Deja Vu (Add a [[Red Seal|]] to selected card in your hand Available from start. ) to put a red seal on an existing 2.

- Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) and Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ): Due to the small size of the deck, the likelihood of stumbling across 2s on these deck is very much better, and thus allows for more efficient scaling.

- Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ): The starting +2 Hand size lets the player see more cards, and also allows for more lenient discarding in certain situations.

- Plasma Deck ( Balance [[Chips|]] and [[Mult|]] when calculating score for played hand <br> base Blind size Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ): Sources of Chips are inherently better on Plasma Deck due to it treating Chips and Mult equally. Before the player gets their +Mult and xMult going, Wee Joker can be an absolute carry, often winning the run by itself.

- Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ): May generate with a higher starting number of 2s, which can make finding and scoring them easier.

### Poker Hands

Hands that score large quantities of 2s are preferable to other hands. Focus on these in the early game:

- Pair and Two Pair (mostly for emergencies)

- Three of a Kind

- Full House

As Deck manipulation access becomes more realistic, pivot to these hands (the final two are the most ideal, as they allow five 2s to score all at once):

- Four of a Kind

- Five of a Kind

- Flush Five

### Jokers

- Any retrigger Jokers work incredibly well with Wee Joker, as rescoring a 2 upgrades Wee Joker another time. In particular, Hack (Retrigger each played , , , ) offers unconditional retriggers to 2s, making it very helpful, with one copy providing up to an extra +40 Chips on a hand with five scoring 2s. Hanging Chad () is also very strong with Wee Joker, guaranteeing +24 Chips if a 2 can be scored. Sock and Buskin () can only retrigger 2s if Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ) is owned. Seltzer () and Dusk () can also be useful, but they have certain limits; Seltzer () only lasts for 10 hands, and Dusk () only works on the final hand.

- Drunkard ( discard each round Common Effect N/A (Passive) ) and Merry Andy ( discards each round, <br> hand size Win a run in or fewer rounds Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): The extra discards provided by these Jokers make finding and scoring 2s much easier.

- Even Steven (Played cards with [[Card Ranks|]] rank give when scored <br> ) and Fibonacci (Each played , , , , ): Giving +4 Mult and +8 Mult, respectively, whenever a 2 is scored, either of these can make playing large quantities of 2s even more rewarding, especially when accounting for retriggers.

- Trading Card (If of round has only card, ) is a source of both money and deck thinning, which helps Wee Joker by making 2s proportionally more common, and making acquiring better scoring easier as the run progresses.

- DNA (If of round has only card, ): By duplicating 2s, especially 2s with a Red Seal, this Joker can scale much faster, making it a near auto-include when discovered while holding this Joker, even if just temporarily.

- Splash (Every counts in scoring Common Effect N/A (Passive) ) allows the player to score 2s without needing to be part of the scoring hand. This is particularly useful if the player only draws one 2, as splash can score the 2 while playing 4 other cards to find more 2s later.

- The Idol (Each played [[Card Ranks|]] of [[Card Suits|]] gives when scored<br><small>Card changes every round</small> In one hand, ) can be an amazing joker to give huge amounts of multiplicative mult if the player has a lot of 2s in their deck. While it requires a massive amount of deck-fixing, it can pay off incredibly well by giving X2 Mult when a 2 is scored, which pairs well with the retriggers that Wee Joker builds often have.

- Ancient Joker (Each played card with [[Card Suits|]] gives when scored, <br> <small>suit changes at end of round</small> ) can potentially be useful if there are a large number of Wild 2s, as this enables the extremely powerful Flush Five - a hand that can guide a run far into Endless Mode in combination with repeated Multiplicative Mult sources and Planet Card scaling. Bloodstone ( ) can be considered in its absence by creating a large number of 2s of  Hearts, albeit its randomness factor makes it a lot less reliable. This isn't an issue if Oops! ### Consumables

- Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) and Strength (Increases rank of up to selected cards by Available from start. ) can make more 2s.

- The Hanged Man (Destroys up to selected cards Available from start. ) can remove non-2s allowing you to see 2s easier.

- Deja Vu (Add a [[Red Seal|]] to selected card in your hand Available from start. ) can apply Red Seals onto 2s allowing Wee Joker to scale faster.

- Cryptid (Create copies of selected card in your hand Available from start. ) can create copies of 2s.

### Vouchers

- Grabber () together with Nacho Tong (Permanently gain an additional hand per round Play a total of 2,) are very good Vouchers to take, as they allow the player to score more 2s, or use the extra hands as pseudo-discards to find more 2s.

- Wasteful (), as well as its upgraded counterpart Recyclomancy (), are good Vouchers to take when using Wee Joker. As scoring non-2s is less ideal, discards can be used in order to locate 2s.

- Hieroglyph ( Ante, <br> ) gives more opportunity to score 2s, but might not be worth it if the player is struggling to score enough points. Its upgraded counterpart, Petroglyph ( Ante, <br> ), may also be worth considering if the player has access to extra discards, but it is much less justifiable on the higher Stakes.

### Boss Blinds

- This Joker is easily distinguishable when played against Amber Acorn ( ), as it retains its unique shape even when flipped face-down.

## Strategy

As skipping is usually a high-risk strategy, it can be slightly tricky to win a run in 18 or fewer rounds, meaning the player must skip at least 6 Blinds, averaging at 1.25 Blinds per Ante skipped.

- A basic Deck, such as Red Deck ( ) or Blue Deck ( hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ), is likely the best, as an additional discard/hand makes it more feasible to play more risky as is the case with skipping.

- Plasma Deck ( Balance [[Chips|]] and [[Mult|]] when calculating score for played hand <br> base Blind size Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) can skip more in the early game due to its balancing ability, making the need to obtain Jokers early less of a priority.

- Anaglyph Deck ( After defeating each [[Blinds|]], gain a [[File:|24px]] [[Tags|]] Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) earns the player a Double Tag ( ) upon defeating a Boss Blind, making skipping slightly more viable.

- Tags in general to look out for are Investment Tag ( ) and Speed Tag ( ), as earning Economy is critical since the player sees fewer Shops and Blind payouts overall.

- Throwback ( for each [[Blinds|]] skipped this run <br> }} ) is basically a must-have during the unlock attempt, as it directly benefits from skipping.

 - The Uncommon Tag ( ) may be a quick way to obtain Throwback early, on top of retroactively scaling it.

## Trivia

- The card art is identical to the default Joker ( ), but scaled down to a smaller size. This also makes all Joker Stickers appear at a scaled-down size on the card itself.

 - When duplicating this joker through the use of Ankh (Create a copy of a random [[Jokers|]], destroy all other Jokers <br> from copy)}} Available from start. ) or Invisible Joker (After rounds, sell this card to a random Joker <br> /2}} <br> ]] from copy)}} Win a run without ever having more than Rare Effect N/A (Passive) ), the Joker Sticker will appear normal sized, going off the card.

- This Joker is part of a set that doesn't adhere to the standard joker size, along with Photograph (), Half Joker ( ), and Square Joker (This Joker gains if played hand has exactly cards <br> }} ).

- The term "wee" is a Scottish term used to refer to something as small-sized or not at a regular size. This also ties into the Wee Joker's ability to keep building +8 Chips for every scoring card with a rank of 2, the smallest rank in Balatro.
