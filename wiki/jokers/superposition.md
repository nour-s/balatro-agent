# Superposition

(Must have room) Copyable Perishable Eternal

Superposition is a Common Effect Joker that will gift you a Tarot card whenever you can play a Straight that contains Aces: either Ace/K/Q/J/10 or Ace/2/3/4/5. This also includes Straight Flush and Royal Flush.

## Synergies

### Decks

- Red Deck ( ) gives an extra discard, allowing the player to search for Straights with an Ace easier.

- Blue Deck ( hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) gives an extra hand, allowing the player to generate more Tarot Cards.

- Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) gives extra hand size, which can help the player with forming Straights with an Ace.

- Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) makes it impossible to get an A/K/Q/J/10 Straight, but about three times easier to assemble an A/2/3/4/5 Straight.

### Poker Hands

- Straight with an Ace.

- Straight Flush (and Royal Flush) with an Ace.

### Jokers

- Shortcut (Allows to be made with gaps of <br> )}} Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): Normally, there are only two possible straights that contain an ace: A/2/3/4/5 and 10/J/Q/K/A. This makes it relatively difficult to trigger Superposition. Shortcut, however, allows straights to skip single cards, vastly increasing the number of ways this joker can be triggered.

- Four Fingers (All and can be made with 4 cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ): Similarly, though to a lesser extent, Four Fingers makes it easier to trigger Superposition by reducing the requirements for a straight to just 4 consecutive cards instead of 5.

 - Mathematically, assuming a standard 52-card deck: the number of 5-card hands that trigger Superposition increases by a factor of 12 with the addition of Four Fingers, a factor of 16 with the addition of Shortcut, and a factor of 84 with both. Meanwhile, the chance of getting any straight at all increases by a factor of 12.2 with the addition of Four Fingers, a factor of 12.8 with the addition of Shortcut, and a factor of 76.3 with both.

 - Be aware that the Ace must score for Superposition to trigger. 5/6/7/8/A will not trigger Superposition with only Four Fingers, though it will with both Four Fingers and Splash (Every counts in scoring Common Effect N/A (Passive) ).

- Jokers that give bonuses from playing Straights, like Devious Joker ( ), Runner (Gains if played hand contains a <br> }} ), Crazy Joker ( ), and The Order ( if played hand contains a Win a run without playing a . ), all work well with Superposition.

- Scholar () and Walkie Talkie (): Will give additional chips and Mult, as Straights triggering the Superposition effect will always contain an Ace, plus a 10 or 4.

### Vouchers

- Grabber () and Nacho Tong (Permanently gain an additional hand per round Play a total of 2,) give additional hands, allowing the player to generate more Tarot Cards.

- Wasteful () and Recyclomancy () give additional discards, allowing the player to search for Straights with an Ace easier.

- Paint Brush ( ) and Palette ( hand size Reduce your hand size down to five cards. ) both increase hand size, which can help players with forming Straights with an Ace.

## Anti-synergies

### Decks

- Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) can make it difficult to use Superposition, as the randomized rank and suit makes it harder to form Straights.

- Black Deck ( Joker slot <br> hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) has slight anti-synergy, as 1 less available hand makes it slightly harder to find Straights. However, it is easier to take Superposition, as it is less likely to take up a crucial Joker slot needed for survival.

- 15 Minute City ( Eternal <br>Eternal Two copies of every face card;<br>no Aces, 2s, or 3s) and Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) are not recommended, as these decks don't contain any Aces by default.

- Five-Card Draw ( discards per round<br> hand size<br> Joker slots <br>Banned Jokers: , , )'s -3 hand size makes it extremely hard to assemble Straights with an Ace.

### Jokers

- Jokers that reduce hand size, like Stuntman (,<br> hand size Earn at least 100 million (100,000,000) Chips in a single hand. ) and Merry Andy ( discards each round, <br> hand size Win a run in or fewer rounds Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ), have anti-synergy with Superposition, as a smaller hand size makes it harder to assemble Straights with an Ace.

### Consumables

- Ectoplasm (Add [[Card Modifiers#Editions|]] to a random hand size Available from start. ) and Ouija (Converts all cards in hand to a single random [[Card Ranks|]]<br> hand size Available from start. ) have anti-synergy with Superposition, as a smaller hand size makes it harder to assemble Straight with an Ace. Not only that, but Ouija (Converts all cards in hand to a single random [[Card Ranks|]]<br> hand size Available from start. ) converts all cards held in hand into one single rank, which makes assembling Straights with an Ace less consistent.

### Blinds

- The Manacle ( )'s -1 hand size makes it hard to assemble Straights with an Ace.

- The Water ( ) will always start with 0 discards, making it very hard to assemble Straights with an Ace.

## Trivia

- In quantum mechanics, superposition is the ability of a quantum system to be in multiple states at the same time until it is measured. In Balatro, Aces can be either high or low depending on the rest of the hand. The sprite of this Joker resembles an electron, which can only have +1/2 or -1/2 spin, similar to how Aces can only form high or low straights.

- The background of the Joker art for Superposition contains a repeating pattern of 𝜓 (psi) and λ→ (lambda with right arrow above). According to LocalThunk, this is a reference to eigenvalue equation A^ψi=λiψi,1] a cornerstone of quantum mechanics about measurement and the nature of quantum states. 𝜓 is the eigenfunction/eigenstate (a specific quantum state of the system), and 𝜆 is the eigenvalue (value of measuring the observable quantity under this state), which could be a vector. Superposition means the system can be in multiple eigenstates at the same time.
