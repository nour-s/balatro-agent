# Fibonacci

Fibonacci is an Uncommon Additive Mult Joker that gives +8 Mult every time an Ace, 2, 3, 5, or 8 scores.

## Synergies

### Decks

- Thanks to the absence of face cards, Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) has a greater concentration of cards favorable by Fibonacci, and thus works really well with it.

- Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) will also work as well, although this deck doesn't start with any Aces.

- Erratic Deck ( All [[Card Ranks|]] and [[Suits|]] in deck are randomized Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) could also contain a great number of Aces, 2s, 3s, 5s or 8s, which is preferred for Fibonacci usage.

### Poker Hands

- As Three of a Kind, Full House and Four of a Kind all utilize a number of the same rank, they often work really well with Fibonacci. Low-ended Straight also makes great use of Fibonacci.

- Flush is difficult early on, but can earn +40 Mult from Fibonacci in an unmodified deck, and diligent suit conversion can make triggering Fibonacci a simple matter of picking ranks.

## Anti-synergies

### Decks

- 15 Minute City ( Eternal <br>Eternal Two copies of every face card;<br>no Aces, 2s, or 3s) doesn't work well with Fibonacci due to lack of Aces, 2s, and 3s by default.

## Trivia

- The Fibonacci sequence is a mathematical pattern where the next number in the pattern is found by adding the two previous numbers together. It can be found in nature, such as in how certain plants flower or fruits grow. If the pattern is started with zero/one, it goes: 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, and so on. This is represented by the ranks Ace (1), 2, 3, 5 and 8 triggering it, its effect of +8 Mult for those cards, and its price of $8.

- The card art shows a Fibonacci spiral, an approximation of the Golden spiral. The distance between the center and the spiral gets multiplied by a certain factor for every quarter turn it makes. This factor approaches the golden ratio (≈1.618) as the Fibonacci numbers approach infinity.
