# Golden Ticket

when scored Copyable Perishable Eternal

Golden Ticket is a Common Economy Joker that gives $4 for every scoring Gold Card ( ), effectively turning them into more powerful Gold Seal () cards.

For this Joker's unlock requirement's "5 card hand", the hand's 5 cards do not need to score. For example, the player can unlock this Joker by playing a High Card hand of one scoring card with four non-scoring cards. All cards do need to be gold cards before any joker effects, so Golden Ticket will not unlock if one or more of the cards was changed to gold by Midas Mask (All played cards become [[Gold Cards|]] ).

## Synergies

### Decks

- The Zodiac Deck ( Start run with , , and Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) works best with Golden Ticket thanks to its increased Tarot card frequency, allowing Gold card conversion more frequent.

### Poker Hands

- Playing 5-card hand such as Flush or Full House with as many Gold cards as possible will ensure maximum income.

### Jokers

- "On scored" retrigger Jokers will reactivate Golden Ticket's effect. So Hanging Chad (), Dusk (), and Seltzer () are great with this Joker. Hack (Retrigger each played , , , ) and Sock and Buskin () can also be great if the Gold cards have the relevant ranks.

- Midas Mask (All played cards become [[Gold Cards|]] ) turns all Face cards into Gold cards when scored. It will be even stronger if paired with Pareidolia (All cards are considered cards Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ), as all cards now can be converted into Gold cards.

- Bull ( for each you have <br> }} ) and Bootstraps ( for every you have <br> }} Have at least Jokers at the same time. ) benefit from the high money making capabilities of this joker.

### Card Modifiers

- A Gold Seal () will earn $3 when scored, giving $7 total per Gold Card.

- A Red Seal () will retrigger Golden Ticket, giving $8 per Gold Card.

### Consumables

- The Devil (Enhances selected card into a [[Card Modifiers|]] Available from start. ) can make more Gold cards.

- Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) can turn some cards into Gold cards.

- The Hanged Man (Destroys up to selected cards Available from start. ) can remove non-Gold cards.

- Deja Vu (Add a [[Red Seal|]] to selected card in your hand Available from start. ) can apply Red Seals onto Gold cards allowing Golden Ticket to generate more money.

## Anti-Synergies

### Jokers

- Vampire (This Joker gains per scoring played, removes card <br> }} ) has anti-synergy, as the Gold Enhancement gets removed before it can score. However if the player is also using Midas Mask (All played cards become [[Gold Cards|]] ) this becomes a non-issue.

 - This innately applies to the Bram Poker ( Jokers no longer appear in the [[The Shop|]] Eternal <br>, <br>, ) as it starts with an Eternal Vampire ().

- Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ) can have anti-synergy, as having more than $4 during a round will disable its effect. It may be better to keep Gold Enhancement Cards in hand and let them trigger when the round ends.

- Mime () has slight anti-synergy, since a Gold Card held in hand with Mime will give $6, more than what Golden Ticket gives for playing Gold Cards.

## Trivia

- The numbers 16180339 used in the artwork are the first digits of the Golden ratio, perhaps because of the shared "golden" thread.

- This Joker is part of a series, along with Stone Joker (Gives for each [[Stone Card|]] in your <br> }} ), Steel Joker (Gives for each [[Steel Cards|]] in your <br> }} ), Glass Joker (This Joker gains for every [[Glass_Cards|]] that is destroyed <br> }} Have at least [[Glass_Cards|]] ) and Lucky Cat (This Joker gains every time a [[Lucky Cards|]] card triggers <br> }} ), where the Joker only appears once players have at least one card with the relevant enhancement in their deck. Interestingly, Golden Ticket is the only Common Joker in this list.

- This Joker has a smaller width than standard Joker cards when face up. However, when it is flipped face-down, it uses the same standard size of card back as the rest. The other Joker with this similar property is Business Card ().
