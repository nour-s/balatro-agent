# 8 Ball

(Must have room) Copyable Perishable Eternal

8 Ball is a Common Effect Joker that has a 1 in 4 chance to generate a Tarot card whenever an 8 is scored.

## Synergies

Compared to other Tarot-generating jokers, 8 Ball is slow to begin with. You would need to draw and play all four 8s in your deck to simply match the expected value of a Cartomancer (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] card when [[Blinds|]] is selected <br> Discover every Tarot Card. ), and even then, it's not unlikely that you'll whiff every trigger and end up with no Tarots for your effort. However, if you're patient enough to play towards it, you'll find it's one of the most effective Tarot generators in the game. Unlike other Jokers of its kind, an 8 Ball at maximum efficiency can create multiple Tarots per hand without copy effects, outpacing even a Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ).

The deck-fixing properties that Tarot cards provide can make it easier to play more 8s and thus recursively create more Tarots. The enhancements can help with scoring as well, making up for the rank's relative lack of scoring Jokers. All of this is especially effective if the player is able to get a Red Seal () on one of their 8s and then repeatedly copy it.

- Pair or Three of a Kind of 8s is good to start.

- Five of a Kind or Flush Five works well once the player has a lot of 8s.

## Anti-Synergies

- Nebula Deck ( Start run with the voucher <br> consumable slot Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] )'s fewer consumable slots makes it harder to hold and use Tarot Cards from 8-Ball.

- 8-Ball may prevent the player from maximizing value from Blue Seals (Creates the card for final played [[Poker Hands|]] of round if in hand<br> ), especially when the tarots generate during the last hand and fill up all consumable slots.

## Trivia

- The visual design of this card is based on the Magic 8 Ball, which itself is based on the 8 ball used in pool or billiards. The user holds the ball with the 8 facing them and asks the Magic 8 Ball a question. They then turn it over, and one of 20 answers (usually a variation on yes, no, or maybe) will appear in a window on the back of the ball.

 - Billiards/Pool is also a common casino game like Poker among various other card games.
