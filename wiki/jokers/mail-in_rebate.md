# Mail-In Rebate

, rank changes every round Copyable Perishable Eternal

Mail-In Rebate is a Common Economy Joker that gives $5 for every card discarded of a certain rank. The rank changes every round, choosing from a random rank in the player's deck (this chooses more common ranks in the deck more often).

## Synergies

### Decks

- Red Deck ( ) grants an extra discard, allowing more cards of the selected rank to be discarded.

- Painted Deck ( hand size, <br> Joker slot Win a run with any [[Decks|deck]] ) allows the player to have more cards in hand, letting the player discard more cards without sacrificing good hands.

- Black Deck ( Joker slot <br> hand every round Discover at least items from your [[collection]] ) can more easily take Mail-In Rebate, as it has more Joker slots, and it is recommended to discard more with Black Deck anyway, as the player must play better hands to compensate for having fewer hands. Black Deck can also hold more Jokers that help with discarding, like Drunkard ( discard each round Common Effect N/A (Passive) ).

- Abandoned Deck ( Start run with no [[Card Ranks#Face cards|]] in your deck Win a run with the on [[Stakes|any difficulty]] ) and Mad World ( Extra no longer earn money<br>Earn no at end of round Eternal, Negative <br>Eternal Ranks 2 through 9 only, 32 cards totalBanned Blinds: ) both contain fewer ranks and total cards, making it easier to find the selected rank and discard it.

### Poker Hands

Hands that need fewer cards are better if the player has a smaller hand size, like Three of a Kind, which allows them to discard 5 cards if they have the default hand size. For this reason, Straight, Flush, and Full House are less valuable with Rebate.

However, Five of a Kind and Flush Five are sometimes better in highly centralized decks, as with more of that rank, Mail-In Rebate will likely choose that rank, and discarding them will be fine, as the player is more likely to draw more to form a Five of a Kind still.

### Jokers

- Jokers that add more discards work great with Mail-In Rebate, those being Drunkard ( discard each round Common Effect N/A (Passive) ) and Merry Andy ( discards each round, <br> hand size Win a run in or fewer rounds Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ). It is recommended to be cautious with Merry Andy, though, as the lowered hand size can sometimes result in the player discarding fewer cards by virtue of wanting to save a strong poker hand.

- Jokers that give an effect when discarding, like Castle (This Joker gains per discarded [[Card Suits|]] card, suit changes every round <br> }} ), Faceless Joker (), and Hit the Road (This Joker gains for every discarded this round<br> }} Discard at the same time. ), synergize well with Mail-In Rebate, as the player can use them to get even more value out of discards.

- Blueprint (Copies ability of [[Jokers|]] ) and Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ) can copy the money generation effect, then copy a different Joker's effect for scoring hands, effectively allowing the player to double Mail-In Rebate's effect for free.

- Sixth Sense (If of round is a single , destroy it and create a [[Spectral Cards|]] card <br> Uncommon Effect N/A (Passive) ) and DNA (If of round has only card, ) usually work well with Rebate, as the player will want to discard to find the card desired to play the first hand. DNA particularly works well, as by adding more cards of the same rank, the chance of Rebate selecting that rank is higher.

### Blinds

- The Hook ( ) discards 2 random cards every hand played, potentially triggering Mail-In Rebate if discarded cards are chosen for this blind.

### Vouchers

- Wasteful () and Recyclomancy () give additional discards, allowing more chances to find the cards of selected rank and generate more money.

## Anti-Synergies

### Decks

- Blast Off ( hands per round<br> discards per round<br> Joker Slots Eternal <br>Eternal <br><br>Banned [[Vouchers]]: , <br>Banned Jokers: ) doesn't work well with Mail-In Rebate, as this deck only starts with 2 discards.

### Jokers

- Any Jokers that benefits from not using Discards, like Delayed Gratification (Earn per if no discards are used by end of the round Common Economy N/A (Passive) ) or Banner ( ), have anti-synergy with Mail-In Rebate.

- Burglar (When [[Blind|]] is selected, ) removes all discards, effectively disabling Mail-In Rebate.

### Blinds

- The Water ( ) sets your discards to 0, making Mail-In Rebate useless for the round.

- Since debuffed cards do not have a rank, any blind that debuffs cards makes it less likely for the player to make use of Mail-In Rebate.

### Stakes

- Blue Stake and above hinder Mail-In Rebate's potential because they have 1 less discard by default.

## Strategy

Mail-In rebate has an extremely high potential cash output. The player should try and maximize its potential by ensuring that all the cards in your deck are the same rank. This can be done through a variety of methods.

Copying Mail-In Rebate with Blueprint (Copies ability of [[Jokers|]] ) or Brainstorm (Copies the ability of leftmost [[Jokers|]] Discard a . ) while discarding 5 cards gives a total of $50, and adding more copies just adds more money per discard.

The player should be careful not to discard too much, as completely running out of cards is an instant loss.

## Trivia

- In real life, a "mail-in rebate" (MIR) is a special type of rebate usually done as a form of sales promotion. Typically, the customer buys a product at full price, and by mailing in a coupon or a receipt to the manufacturer, they receive a partial refund (or in some cases a full refund) on the product. This mirrors the Joker's ability in Balatro. By purchasing Mail-In Rebate and discarding certain cards, players can get a refund on their purchase.

- This Joker is addressed to Jerry Seinfeld's apartment from the show Seinfeld.

- If the deck has no card with any rank (i.e., full Stone Cards (<br>)), Mail-In Rebate will default to +$5 per discarded Ace.
