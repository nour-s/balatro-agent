# Lucky Cat

card successfully triggers
 (Currently X1 Mult) Copyable Perishable Eternal

Lucky Cat is an Uncommon scaling Multiplicative Mult Joker that gains X0.25 Mult each time a Lucky Card ( chance for <br> ) successfully triggers, and starts at X1 Mult. A card that triggers both the Money and Mult effects at the same time will only count as one activation for Lucky Cat.

Lucky Cat will only appear in the shop after you have at least one Lucky Card in your deck.

### Poker Hands

While Lucky Cat technically affects all hands evenly, five-card hands allow more Lucky Cards to be played at once, increasing the potential for a successful trigger, such as:

- Straight

- Flush

- Full House

- Red Seals () double the opportunities for the Lucky Card to trigger.

### Consumables

- The Magician (Enhances selected cards to [[Card Modifiers|]] Available from start. ) can make more Lucky Cards.

- Death (Select cards, convert the card into the card<br> Available from start. ) can turn some cards into Lucky Cards.

- Deja Vu (Add a [[Red Seal|]] to selected card in your hand Available from start. ) can apply Red Seals onto Lucky Cards, increasing their chance to activate.

## Anti-synergies

### Decks

- Bram Poker ( Jokers no longer appear in the [[The Shop|]] Eternal <br>, <br>, ) makes Lucky Cat useless due to the Eternal Vampire (This Joker gains per scoring played, removes card <br> }} ) this deck starts with.

### Jokers

- Vampire (This Joker gains per scoring played, removes card <br> }} ) is not recommended, as it removes the Lucky enhancement when scored, making Lucky Cat impossible to scale.

- Midas Mask (All played cards become [[Gold Cards|]] ) converts all face cards into Gold Cards ( ), therefore overriding the previous Enhancement.

- Vagabond (Create a [[Tarot Cards|]] ) may be risky to use, as just one $20 trigger will prevent it from creating more Tarot Cards (unless the player has $-16 or less, which is pretty unlikely).

### Blinds

- The Pillar ( ) debuffs previously played cards of the Ante, which may make some Lucky Cards unusable.

- The Fish ( ) can potentially make it hard to play hands with Lucky Cards if multiple hands are required to defeat the Boss Blind.

## Trivia

- Many Asian countries have superstitions about cats being lucky, specifically the Maneki-neko (or 'waving cat'), which is a figurine based on the calico Japanese Bobtail cat breed, which is believed to give good luck to the owner.

- This Joker is part of a series, along with Stone Joker (Gives for each [[Stone Card|]] in your <br> }} ), Steel Joker (Gives for each [[Steel Cards|]] in your <br> }} ), Glass Joker (This Joker gains for every [[Glass_Cards|]] that is destroyed <br> }} Have at least [[Glass_Cards|]] ), and Golden Ticket (Played [[Gold Cards|]] ), where the Joker only appears once players have at least one card with the relevant enhancement in their deck.
